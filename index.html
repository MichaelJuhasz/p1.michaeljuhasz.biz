Bio

Sometimes I live in the country

But the Charles just isn't the Mississippi, or even the Wakonda Auga River and my father gave a lot more inches than Henry Stamper. So it was with as long a leash as you can imagine that I roamed far from my father's field of software engineering, ranged over and then down into subjects less studied by my most immediate relatives; mostly religious studies, in that social scientific sense. Which is how I wound up on this side of the country and in a more round about way, at this desk at a library on the other side of the Harvard campus.

A very long leash perhaps, but those family ties do bind, and even if they're not barbed and spined, they can hold us close forever. For that reason, or perhaps because I thought it might be necessary, in this life or the next, to have something like a marketable skill set, I thought I'd give computer science a try.
